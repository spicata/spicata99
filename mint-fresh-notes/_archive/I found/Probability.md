$$P(A\cap B)=P(A|B)\times P(B)$$
$$P(A|B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}$$
$$P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cup B)$$

---

Independence: $P(A|B)=P(A)$

> [!Question] If $P(A)=x$ and $P(A\cup B)=y$, find $P(B)$
> $$P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)$$
> $$x=y+P(B)-yP(B)$$
> $$x-y=(1-y)P(B)$$
> $$\frac{x-y}{1-y}=P(B)$$

---

Mutually exclusive: $P(A\cap B)=0$
